## 1. Purpose

To provide safe, confidential, and (where permitted) anonymous channels for raising concerns about suspected wrongdoing, to ensure concerns are investigated fairly, and to protect those who speak up in good faith from retaliation.

## 2. Scope

All employees, contractors, officers, directors, and (where applicable) third parties, regarding suspected misconduct, illegality, fraud, financial-crime facilitation, safety, or policy breaches.

## 5. Reporting Channels

Multiple channels are provided: line management, a confidential email/hotline, and an independent third-party intake where available. Channels accept anonymous reports where permitted by law. Channels independent of line management exist for concerns involving senior management.

## 6. Confidentiality & Anonymity

Reporter identity is protected to the fullest extent possible and shared strictly on a need-to-know basis. Anonymous reports are accepted and investigated as far as the available information allows.

## 7. Investigation Procedure

1. Acknowledge receipt (where contactable).
2. Triage for seriousness and independence (no conflicts).
3. Assign an Investigation Lead.
4. Investigate fairly, gather evidence, and protect all parties' rights.
5. Determine findings and remediation.
6. Report outcomes to the ARC; provide feedback to the reporter where possible.

## 8. Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith or participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited and is itself a disciplinary offence. Suspected retaliation is investigated as a serious matter.

## 9. Oversight & Records

The ARC receives periodic reporting on the volume, themes, and outcomes of reports. Records are kept confidential and retained per the Records Management schedule (Policy the relevant policy).

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

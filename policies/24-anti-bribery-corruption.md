## 1. Purpose

To prohibit bribery and corruption in all forms and ensure Aspire, its staff, and those acting on its behalf comply with applicable anti-corruption laws, including a controlled gifts & hospitality regime and third-party due diligence.

## 2. Scope

All employees, contractors, officers, directors, agents, intermediaries, and business partners acting for Aspire, in all markets.

| Term | Definition |

## 5. Prohibitions

Aspire prohibits: bribery and kickbacks (giving or receiving); facilitation payments; improper political or charitable contributions used to gain advantage; and any improper advantage to/from public officials or private parties. Zero tolerance applies regardless of local custom or perceived business need.

## 6. Gifts & Hospitality Register (Procedure)

Gifts and hospitality must be reasonable, proportionate, transparent, infrequent, and never intended to influence a decision. Items above the defined risk threshold require pre-approval; all reportable gifts/hospitality given or received are logged in the **Gifts & Hospitality Register** with giver/recipient, value, purpose, and approval. Cash or cash-equivalents are prohibited.

## 7. Third-Party & Intermediary Due Diligence

Agents, intermediaries, and high-risk partners are risk-assessed for ABC exposure before engagement; contracts include ABC representations, audit rights, and termination for breach (Policy the relevant policy).

## 8. Facilitation Payments & Duress

Facilitation payments are prohibited. Where a payment is demanded under duress (safety risk), staff may comply to protect personal safety and must report it immediately for recording and review.

## 9. Reporting & Investigation

Suspected bribery/corruption is reported via line management or the whistleblowing channel (Policy the relevant policy); reports are investigated confidentially; substantiated breaches lead to disciplinary action and possible referral to authorities.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

## 1. Purpose

To define how Aspire identifies and verifies customers and their beneficial owners, and assesses customer risk, supporting the AML/CFT Policy (12). KYB is central to Aspire because customers are predominantly businesses with layered ownership structures.

## 2. Scope

All customers (businesses and the associated individuals — directors, authorised users, beneficial owners) onboarded to any Aspire product.

## 5. Customer Identification Program (Procedure)

For **business customers (KYB)**, collect and verify: legal name, registration number, registered address; certificate of incorporation / business registration; nature and purpose of business; authorised representatives/signatories; and source of funds/wealth where risk-warranted.

For **individuals (KYC)** (directors, UBOs, authorised users): full name, date of birth, nationality, residential address; government-issued identification; and identity verification (documentary and/or electronic/biometric).

## 6. Beneficial Ownership

- Identify and verify UBOs — typically those owning/controlling **≥ 25%** (or lower per local rules) — and understand the ownership and control structure.
- Identify persons exercising control through other means (e.g. senior managing officials where no UBO is identified).
- Unwrap layered structures (holding companies, trusts, nominees) to the natural persons.

## 7. Customer Risk Rating

- Assign a risk rating (Low/Medium/High) based on customer type, industry, geography, products, ownership complexity, and channel.
- High-risk customers (incl. PEPs) require Enhanced Due Diligence and senior approval to onboard (PEP & EDD Procedure the relevant policy).

## 8. Verification Standards

Use reliable, independent source documents, data, or electronic verification; detect and reject forged, altered, or inconsistent documentation; document the verification method and result.

## 9. Ongoing Due Diligence

- Monitor activity for consistency with the expected customer profile.
- Conduct periodic KYC refresh by risk (High: annual; Medium: 2 yrs; Low: 3–5 yrs) and on trigger events.
- Keep customer information current.

## 10. Screening

Screen customers and UBOs against sanctions, PEP, and adverse-media lists at onboarding and on an ongoing basis (Policy 12 §8; Sanctions the relevant policy).

## 11. Reliance on Third Parties

Where third parties perform elements of CDD, Aspire remains responsible, must obtain the underlying data without delay, and must ensure standards are met (FATF Rec. 17).

## 12. Failure to Complete CDD

If satisfactory CDD cannot be completed, Aspire does not onboard / terminates the relationship and considers filing an STR (no "tipping off").

## 13. Record Keeping

KYC/KYB records are retained for the statutory period (e.g. **≥ 5 years** after the relationship ends) (Records Management the relevant policy).

## 15. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

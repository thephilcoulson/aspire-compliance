## 1. Purpose

To define how Aspire verifies the identity of customers and beneficial owners and assesses customer risk, supporting the AML/CFT Policy (Policy 12).

## 2. Scope

All customers (businesses and associated individuals) onboarded to Aspire's products.

## 3. Customer Identification Program

For **business customers (KYB)**, collect and verify:
- Legal name, registration number, and registered address
- Certificate of incorporation / business registration
- Nature and purpose of business
- Authorised representatives / signatories
- Source of funds / source of wealth where risk-warranted

For **individuals (KYC)** (directors, UBOs, authorised users):
- Full name, date of birth, nationality, residential address
- Government-issued identification
- Identity verification (documentary and/or electronic/biometric)

## 4. Beneficial Ownership

- Identify and verify Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs), typically those owning/controlling **≥ 25%** (or lower per local rules), and understand ownership/control structure.
- Identify persons exercising control through other means.

## 5. Customer Risk Rating

- Assign a risk rating (e.g. Low/Medium/High) based on customer type, industry, geography, products, and channel.
- High-risk customers (incl. PEPs) require Enhanced Due Diligence and senior approval to onboard.

## 6. Verification Standards

- Use reliable, independent source documents, data, or electronic verification.
- Detect and reject forged or inconsistent documentation.

## 7. Ongoing Due Diligence

- Monitor customer activity for consistency with the expected profile.
- Conduct periodic KYC refresh based on risk (e.g. High: annual; Medium: [2 yrs]; Low: [3–5 yrs]) and on trigger events.
- Keep customer information current.

## 8. Screening

- Screen customers and UBOs against sanctions, PEP, and adverse-media lists at onboarding and ongoing (link to Policy 12 §6).

## 9. Reliance on Third Parties

- Where third parties perform CDD, Aspire remains responsible and must obtain underlying data and ensure standards are met.

## 10. Failure to Complete CDD

- If satisfactory CDD cannot be completed, do not onboard / terminate the relationship and consider filing an STR.

## 11. Record Keeping

- KYC/KYB records retained for the statutory period (e.g. **≥ 5 years** after relationship ends).

## 12. Enforcement & Review

Breaches may carry regulatory and criminal consequences. Reviewed at least annually.

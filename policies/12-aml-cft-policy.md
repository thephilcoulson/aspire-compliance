## 1. Purpose

To prevent Aspire's products and services from being used for money laundering, terrorism financing, proliferation financing, or sanctions evasion, and to ensure compliance with applicable AML/CFT laws, the FATF standards, and regulator expectations (notably MAS Notice PSN01 in Singapore). This policy is the apex AML document; KYC/KYB (13), Transaction Monitoring (see Policy Index), PEP/EDD (see Policy Index), and Sanctions (see Policy Index) sit beneath it.

## 2. Scope

All business lines, customers, employees, directors, and transactions across all jurisdictions in which Aspire operates.

## 3. Definitions & Glossary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Money Laundering** | Processing criminal proceeds to disguise their illegal origin. |
| **Terrorism Financing (TF)** | Providing funds for terrorist acts/organisations. |
| **Proliferation Financing (PF)** | Financing the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction. |
| **CDD / EDD / SDD** | Customer / Enhanced / Simplified Due Diligence. |
| **PEP** | Politically Exposed Person (see PEP/EDD Procedure the relevant policy). |

| **Tipping-off** | Improperly disclosing that an STR has or may be filed. |
| **Beneficial Owner** | The natural person who ultimately owns or controls a customer. |

## 4. Governance

- The **Board / Audit & Risk Committee** holds ultimate accountability and approves the AML/CFT program and risk appetite.
- The **MLRO** owns the program, is the point of contact for regulators and the FIU, and has the authority and independence to file STRs without management interference.
- A **deputy MLRO** is appointed for continuity.

## 6. Risk-Based Approach (Procedure)

- An enterprise-wide AML/CFT risk assessment is conducted and maintained, covering customer, product, channel, geographic, and transaction risk.
- Controls are calibrated to assessed risk and reviewed at least annually and on material change (new products, markets, typologies).

## 7. Customer Due Diligence

- CDD/KYC is performed at onboarding and on an ongoing basis (Policy 13).
- **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** applies to higher-risk customers — PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex/opaque ownership, high-risk industries — per the PEP & EDD Procedure (see Policy Index).
- **Simplified Due Diligence** only where permitted and risk is demonstrably low.
- No anonymous or fictitious-name accounts.

## 8. Sanctions & Watchlist Screening

Customers, beneficial owners, and counterparties are screened against applicable sanctions lists (UN, OFAC, EU, MAS, and other relevant lists) and PEP/adverse-media lists at onboarding and on an ongoing basis; true matches are escalated, prohibited dealings blocked and reported. Detailed controls are in the Sanctions, Export Controls & Embargoes policy (see Policy Index).

## 9. Transaction Monitoring

Automated and manual monitoring detects unusual or suspicious activity against defined rules, thresholds, and typologies; alerts are investigated, documented, and dispositioned within defined SLAs (see Transaction Monitoring Standard the relevant policy).

## 10. Suspicious Transaction Reporting (Procedure)

- Staff escalate suspicions internally to the MLRO without "tipping off" the customer.
- The MLRO assesses and, where warranted, files STRs with the relevant FIU (e.g. STRO in Singapore) within statutory timeframes.
- **Tipping-off is strictly prohibited** and may be a criminal offence.

## 11. Record Keeping

CDD records, transaction records, and STR-related documentation are retained for the statutory period (e.g. **at least 5 years**) and made available to regulators on request (Records Management the relevant policy).

## 12. Training

All relevant staff receive AML/CFT training on onboarding and at least annually; specialised training for high-risk roles; completion is tracked (Compliance Training the relevant policy).

## 13. Independent Audit

The AML/CFT program is subject to independent review/audit to assess design and operating effectiveness; findings are reported to the Board (Internal Audit the relevant policy).

## 14. Proliferation & Terrorism Financing

Controls explicitly address TF and PF risks consistent with FATF and local requirements, including targeted financial sanctions obligations.

## 16. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

## 1. Purpose

To prevent Aspire's products and services from being used for money laundering, terrorism financing, proliferation financing, or sanctions evasion, and to ensure compliance with applicable AML/CFT laws and regulator expectations.

## 2. Scope

All business lines, customers, employees, and transactions across all jurisdictions in which Aspire operates.

## 3. Governance

- **Board** holds ultimate accountability and approves the AML/CFT program and risk appetite.
- **MLRO** owns the program, is the point of contact for regulators and FIUs, and has authority and independence to file suspicious transaction reports (STRs).
- A deputy MLRO is appointed for continuity.

## 4. Risk-Based Approach

- An enterprise-wide AML/CFT risk assessment is conducted and maintained, covering customer, product, channel, geographic, and transaction risk.
- Controls are calibrated to assessed risk and reviewed at least annually.

## 5. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

- CDD/KYC is performed at onboarding and on an ongoing basis (see KYC/KYB Policy 13).
- **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)** for high-risk customers, including Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), high-risk jurisdictions, and complex ownership structures.
- **Simplified Due Diligence** only where permitted and risk is demonstrably low.
- No anonymous or fictitious-name accounts.

## 6. Sanctions & Watchlist Screening

- Customers and counterparties are screened against applicable sanctions lists (e.g. UN, OFAC, EU, MAS) and PEP/adverse-media lists at onboarding and on an ongoing basis.
- Positive/true matches are escalated; prohibited dealings are blocked and reported.

## 7. Transaction Monitoring

- Automated and manual monitoring detects unusual or suspicious activity against defined rules and thresholds.
- Alerts are investigated, documented, and dispositioned within defined SLAs.

## 8. Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)

- Staff must escalate suspicions internally to the MLRO without "tipping off" the customer.
- The MLRO assesses and, where warranted, files STRs with the relevant FIU (e.g. STRO in Singapore) within statutory timeframes.
- Tipping-off is strictly prohibited.

## 9. Record Keeping

- CDD records, transaction records, and STR-related documentation are retained for the statutory period (e.g. **at least 5 years**) and made available to regulators on request.

## 10. Training

- All relevant staff receive AML/CFT training on onboarding and at least annually; specialised training for high-risk roles. Completion is tracked.

## 11. Independent Audit

- The AML/CFT program is subject to independent review/audit to assess effectiveness, with findings reported to the Board.

## 12. Proliferation & Terrorism Financing

- Controls explicitly address terrorism financing and proliferation financing risks consistent with FATF and local requirements.

## 13. Enforcement & Review

Breaches may result in disciplinary, regulatory, and criminal consequences. Reviewed at least annually and upon regulatory change.

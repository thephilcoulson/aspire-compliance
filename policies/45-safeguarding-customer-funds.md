## 1. Purpose

To ensure customer funds (e-money and relevant payment balances) are safeguarded in line with the Payment Services Act and equivalent rules, segregated from Aspire's own funds, and reconciled, and that Aspire maintains required capital/financial resources.

## 2. Scope

All customer funds held or controlled by Aspire in connection with payment/e-money services, and Aspire's regulatory capital obligations, across applicable markets.

## 5. Segregation & Safeguarding Method

Customer funds are segregated from Aspire's own funds and safeguarded using an approved method (e.g. holding in a designated trust/safeguarding account with an approved institution, or an approved insurance/guarantee arrangement) consistent with the Payment Services Act and equivalent rules. Safeguarded funds are not used for Aspire's operating purposes and are protected from corporate creditors.

## 6. Reconciliation

Customer balances are reconciled to safeguarded funds at least **daily**, with breaks investigated and resolved promptly and any shortfall topped up immediately. Reconciliation results are reviewed and evidenced (maker-checker).

## 7. Capital & Financial Resources

Aspire monitors and maintains the regulatory capital/financial resources required by its licence(s) (Policy 17), with early-warning thresholds above the minimum and escalation to the Board if approached.

## 8. Governance & Reporting

Safeguarding and capital positions are reported to management and the Board/ARC; breaches or near-breaches are escalated and reported to the regulator as required (Policy 36).

## 9. Assurance

Safeguarding arrangements are independently reviewed/audited (internal and, where required, external/auditor attestation) to confirm segregation, adequacy, and compliance with regulatory requirements.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

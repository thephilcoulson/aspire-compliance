## 1. Purpose

To ensure Aspire does not facilitate transactions or relationships that breach applicable sanctions, targeted financial sanctions, export-control, or embargo regimes, and that potential matches are screened, escalated, frozen, and reported correctly.

## 2. Scope

All customers, beneficial owners, counterparties, transactions, and jurisdictions; all employees and systems involved in onboarding and payments.

| Term | Definition |

## 5. List Management & Screening Coverage

Aspire screens against UN, OFAC (SDN/consolidated), EU, UK (OFSI), MAS, and other relevant lists, plus internal lists. Lists are updated promptly (ideally same-day on publication). Screening covers customers and beneficial owners at onboarding, on list updates (rescreening), and counterparties/parties at payment time, including name, alias, DOB, nationality, and address matching with appropriate fuzzy-matching thresholds.

## 6. Alert Investigation Procedure

1. Alert generated by the screening engine.
2. Analyst reviews against the underlying list entry and customer data.
3. Disposition as false positive (documented) or potential true match (escalated).
4. For potential true matches, freeze/hold pending determination; do not tip off.
5. Confirmed matches: freeze assets, block dealings, and report to the relevant authority within statutory timeframes.

## 7. Export Controls & Dual-Use Considerations

Where Aspire's services could facilitate dealings touching export-controlled goods/technology or sanctioned sectors, transactions are assessed against export-control and sectoral-sanctions obligations; high-risk activity is escalated to Legal/MLRO.

## 8. Prohibited Jurisdictions & Embargoes

Aspire maintains a list of prohibited and high-risk jurisdictions. Dealings with comprehensively embargoed jurisdictions are prohibited. Geographic risk feeds the AML/CFT risk assessment (Policy 12).

## 9. Freezing, Blocking & Reporting

On a confirmed match, funds are frozen and dealings blocked immediately; no funds are released without authority approval. Reports are made to the relevant authority (e.g. MAS, and via STR to STRO where applicable) within required timeframes, with full records retained.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

## 1. Purpose

To operationalise data subject rights and Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs), supporting the Data Privacy & Protection Policy (Policy 04).

## 2. Scope

All processing of personal data by Aspire and all requests from individuals exercising their privacy rights.

## 3. Definitions

- **Data Subject Request (DSR):** A request to exercise a privacy right (access, correction, erasure, etc.).
- **DPIA:** Data Protection Impact Assessment for high-risk processing.

## 5. Data Subject Rights Handling

Aspire honours applicable rights:
- **PDPA:** Access and correction.
- **GDPR:** Access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection, and rights relating to automated decision-making.

Process:
1. **Receipt & logging:** DSRs are logged centrally upon receipt via any channel.
2. **Identity verification:** The requester's identity is verified before disclosure.
3. **Assessment:** Validity, scope, and exemptions are assessed.
4. **Fulfilment:** Response within statutory timeframes — PDPA access within 30 days (or notify of timeline); GDPR within 1 month (extendable by 2 months for complexity, with notice).
5. **Recording:** Outcome is documented.

- Fees are not charged except where permitted (e.g. manifestly unfounded/excessive requests).
- Refusals are justified and communicated with the individual's right to complain to the regulator.

## 6. DPIA Procedure

- A DPIA is conducted for processing likely to result in high risk (e.g. large-scale profiling, sensitive data, new technologies, AI-driven decisions).
- DPIA includes: description of processing, necessity/proportionality assessment, risks to individuals, and mitigations.
- High residual risk requires DPO advice and, under GDPR, prior consultation with the supervisory authority.
- DPIAs are reviewed when processing changes.

## 7. Records of Processing

- A Record of Processing Activities (RoPA) is maintained and kept current.

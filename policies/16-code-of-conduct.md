## 1. Purpose

To set the standards of ethical behaviour and integrity expected of everyone at Aspire, and to protect the Company, its customers, its partners, and its people. The Code is the foundation on which Aspire's conduct, anti-bribery, conflicts, and whistleblowing controls are built.

## 2. Scope

All employees, contractors, officers, and directors, and (by reference in contracts) key third parties acting for Aspire.

| Term | Definition |

## 5. Core Principles

- **Integrity:** Act honestly and ethically in all dealings.
- **Compliance:** Follow all applicable laws, regulations, and Company policies.
- **Respect:** Maintain a workplace free from harassment and discrimination.
- **Accountability:** Take responsibility for actions and decisions.
- **Customer trust:** Treat customers fairly and protect their data, funds, and interests.

## 6. Conflicts of Interest

Disclose actual or potential conflicts (financial interests, outside roles, related-party dealings); do not let personal interests improperly influence business decisions. Detailed handling is in the Conflicts of Interest policy (see Policy Index).

## 7. Anti-Bribery & Corruption

Zero tolerance for bribery, kickbacks, facilitation payments, or improper payments to/from anyone, including public officials. Gifts and hospitality must be reasonable, transparent, and within policy limits and recorded in the gifts & hospitality register. Comply with the Singapore Prevention of Corruption Act, UK Bribery Act, and FCPA where relevant (Anti-Bribery & Corruption policy the relevant policy).

## 8. Fair Dealing & Competition

Compete fairly; do not engage in anti-competitive conduct or misuse others' confidential information.

## 9. Confidentiality & Data Protection

Protect Company, customer, and employee confidential data per the Data Classification (03) and Privacy (04) policies.

## 10. Insider Information

Do not misuse material non-public information for personal gain or share it improperly.

## 15. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

## 1. Purpose

To ensure personnel understand and fulfil their security, privacy, and conduct responsibilities before, during, and after employment, reducing the risk of insider error or misconduct affecting Aspire's customers, funds, and data.

## 2. Scope

All employees, contractors, secondees, interns, and temporary staff, and (by reference) third-party personnel managed under the Vendor Risk Policy (07).

## 5. Before Employment (Procedure)

- **Screening:** Background checks (identity, right-to-work, education/employment, and — where lawful and role-appropriate — criminal and financial/credit checks) proportionate to role sensitivity. **Enhanced screening** for roles with access to Restricted data, financial controls, or regulatory ("fit & proper") significance.
- **Terms:** Employment agreements include confidentiality (NDA), security responsibilities, acceptable use, intellectual-property assignment, and post-termination obligations.

## 6. During Employment

- **Onboarding:** Security, privacy, Code of Conduct, and AML/CFT (where relevant) training completed within first 30 days; acknowledgement recorded.
- **Ongoing training:** Annual refresher and role-specific training; phishing simulations; awareness campaigns.
- **Performance & conduct:** Security and conduct expectations are reinforced; concerns escalated.
- **Disciplinary process:** A formal, fair, documented process applies to security and conduct violations, consistent across the workforce.

## 7. Access & Role Changes

Access is provisioned per the Access Control Policy (02) and adjusted promptly on role change to prevent privilege accumulation.

## 8. Termination & Offboarding (Procedure)

- Access revoked within defined SLA; immediate for involuntary terminations.
- Return of all Company assets (devices, tokens, access cards).
- Reminder of continuing confidentiality and post-employment obligations.
- Exit checklist completed, signed, and retained as evidence.

## 9. Contractors & Third-Party Personnel

Subject to equivalent screening, confidentiality, and security obligations, managed under the Vendor & Third-Party Risk Policy (07).

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

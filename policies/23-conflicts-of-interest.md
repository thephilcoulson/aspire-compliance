## 1. Purpose

To identify, disclose, assess, and manage actual, potential, and perceived conflicts of interest so that decisions are made in the best interests of Aspire and its customers, free from improper personal influence.

## 2. Scope

All employees, contractors, officers, and directors, and all business decisions, procurement, hiring, and customer dealings.

## 5. Types of Conflicts

Common conflicts include: outside business activities and second jobs; personal investments in customers, vendors, or competitors; related-party transactions; hiring or managing relatives; gifts and hospitality (Policy the relevant policy); and personal relationships affecting objectivity.

## 6. Disclosure Procedure

1. Disclose any actual/potential/perceived conflict at onboarding, annually, and immediately on arising.
2. Record the disclosure in the conflicts register.
3. Manager/CCO assesses and decides mitigation.
4. Implement mitigation (recusal, reassignment, information barrier, divestment, or approval with conditions).
5. Monitor and review.

## 7. Mitigation Measures

Mitigations are proportionate to the conflict and may include recusal from decisions, reassignment of responsibilities, information barriers, enhanced oversight, or, where unmanageable, prohibition of the conflicting activity.

## 8. Procurement & Hiring Conflicts

Staff must recuse from procurement, vendor selection, or hiring decisions involving related parties; such decisions require independent approval.

## 9. Board & Executive Conflicts

Directors and executives disclose conflicts to the Board/Governance; conflicted individuals recuse from related deliberations and votes, recorded in minutes.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

## 1. Purpose

To ensure customers are treated fairly throughout their relationship with Aspire and that complaints are handled promptly, fairly, consistently, and used to drive improvement — supporting good conduct outcomes and regulatory expectations on fair dealing.

## 2. Scope

All customer-facing products, communications, and complaint channels across all markets.

## 5. Fair Treatment Principles

Aspire commits to: clear and not-misleading communications; products suitable for the target market; transparent pricing and terms; accessible support; and additional care for vulnerable customers. Fair-dealing outcomes are considered in product design, marketing (Policy the relevant policy), and servicing.

## 6. Complaint Channels & Logging

Complaints can be raised through all customer channels and are logged centrally with a unique reference, category, and severity. Every complaint is acknowledged within one business day.

## 7. Investigation & Resolution Procedure

1. Acknowledge and classify.
2. Investigate impartially, gathering facts.
3. Determine outcome and any redress.
4. Communicate the outcome clearly, with reasons and next steps (including external escalation/ombudsman options where applicable).
5. Resolve within target TAT (14 days); escalate complex cases.

## 8. Root-Cause Analysis & Improvement

Complaints are analysed for systemic root causes; recurring issues drive product/process fixes owned by the relevant business team and tracked to closure. Themes are reported to management and Compliance.

## 9. Vulnerable Customers

Staff are trained to identify and support vulnerable customers, applying additional flexibility and care, and recording the support provided.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

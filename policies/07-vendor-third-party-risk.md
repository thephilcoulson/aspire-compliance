## 1. Purpose

To manage risks arising from third parties and outsourcing arrangements, ensuring vendors meet Aspire's security, privacy, and regulatory requirements.

## 2. Scope

All third parties that process Company or customer data, provide critical services, or connect to Company systems — including SaaS, cloud, payment partners, and sub-processors.

## 3. Risk-Based Tiering

| Tier | Criteria | Diligence |
|------|----------|-----------|
| Critical | Access to Restricted data or critical service dependency | Full security & financial due diligence, on-site/SOC 2/ISO evidence, contract + SLA |
| High | Access to Confidential/personal data | Security questionnaire, certifications review, DPA |
| Standard | Limited/no sensitive data | Baseline review |

## 4. Due Diligence

- Assess security posture (certifications: ISO 27001, SOC 2, PCI-DSS as relevant), financial stability, regulatory standing, and data handling.
- For payment/financial outsourcing, ensure compliance with applicable regulator outsourcing requirements (e.g. MAS Guidelines on Outsourcing).

## 5. Contracts

Contracts with in-scope vendors must include:
- Confidentiality and data protection (DPA where personal data is processed)
- Security control requirements and right to audit
- Breach notification obligations
- Sub-processor controls
- Service levels and exit/termination provisions

## 6. Ongoing Monitoring

- Periodic reassessment based on tier (Critical: annual; High: annual/biennial).
- Track certification renewals, incidents, and SLA performance.
- Maintain a current vendor inventory and sub-processor list.

## 7. Offboarding

- Ensure secure return or destruction of Company data and revocation of access at contract end.

## 8. Concentration & Continuity Risk

- Identify critical-service concentration; ensure continuity/exit plans for key dependencies (link to Policy 09).

## 9. Enforcement & Review

Non-compliant vendors are remediated or offboarded. Reviewed at least annually.

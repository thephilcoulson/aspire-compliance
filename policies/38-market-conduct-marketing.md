## 1. Purpose

To ensure Aspire's marketing, advertising, and customer communications are clear, fair, not misleading, and compliant with financial-promotion and consumer-protection rules across all markets, and that conduct in the market is fair and transparent.

## 2. Scope

All marketing and advertising content, financial promotions, product disclosures, and customer communications across all channels and markets.

## 5. Communication Standards

All customer-facing communications must be clear, fair, and not misleading: accurate, balanced (benefits and risks/fees), substantiated, and appropriate to the target market. Prominent disclosure of material terms, fees, and risks is required; small-print contradictions of headline claims are prohibited.

## 6. Review & Approval Workflow

Regulated promotions and material customer communications are reviewed and approved by Compliance before publication. A record of approvals and the substantiation for claims is retained (Policy 26).

## 7. Claims & Substantiation

Performance, comparison, and 'free/best/guaranteed' claims must be substantiated and current. Rates, fees, and eligibility are stated accurately with required qualifiers.

## 8. Channels, Endorsements & Influencers

Social media, affiliate, and influencer promotions must identify themselves as advertising, comply with the same standards, and be governed by agreements requiring compliance; affiliates/influencers are monitored.

## 9. Monitoring & Remediation

Live communications are monitored; non-compliant content is corrected or withdrawn promptly, with root-cause feedback. Complaints about communications feed the conduct program (Policy 22).

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

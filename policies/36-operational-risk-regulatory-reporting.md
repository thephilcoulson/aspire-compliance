## 1. Purpose

To manage operational risk across Aspire and ensure accurate, timely regulatory and incident reporting to authorities, protecting customers and the Company from losses arising from inadequate or failed processes, people, systems, or external events.

## 2. Scope

All business and support functions, processes, systems, and the regulatory reporting obligations of each market.

## 5. Operational Risk Framework

Operational risk is identified and assessed through RCSAs, captured in the risk register (Policy 15), and monitored via KRIs. Each material process has documented controls with owners. Risks above tolerance require treatment or documented acceptance.

## 6. Loss-Event & Near-Miss Reporting

Operational loss events and near-misses are recorded in a central database with cause, impact, and remediation; analysis drives control improvements and informs capital/risk assessments.

## 7. Key Risk Indicators

KRIs are defined for priority operational risks (e.g. system availability, payment failure rates, processing backlogs, staffing) with thresholds that trigger escalation and action.

## 8. Regulatory Reporting Procedure

1. Maintain a regulatory reporting calendar mapping each return to an owner and deadline.
2. Prepare returns with maker-checker review for accuracy.
3. Submit on time through approved channels.
4. Retain evidence of submission.
Key reporting deadlines (illustrative, to be confirmed per regulator): MAS incident reporting within **1 hour** of discovery for relevant incidents; privacy breach notification per Policy 04; periodic financial/AML returns per licence conditions (Policy 17).

## 9. Incident & Regulator Notification

Reportable incidents (major service disruption, security breaches, fraud thresholds, control failures) are notified to the relevant regulator within required timeframes, coordinated with Incident Response (Policy 08) and Legal/Compliance.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

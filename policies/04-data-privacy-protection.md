## 1. Purpose

To define how Aspire collects, uses, discloses, stores, and protects personal data in compliance with the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA), the EU GDPR where applicable, and other relevant privacy laws.

## 2. Scope

All personal data processed by Aspire about customers, prospects, employees, and third parties, in any jurisdiction.

## 3. Definitions

- **Personal Data:** Data about an identified or identifiable individual.
- **Processing:** Any operation performed on personal data.
- **Data Subject:** The individual to whom personal data relates.
- **Controller / Processor:** Roles as defined under GDPR/PDPA.

## 4. Data Protection Principles

1. **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** — process on a valid legal basis with clear notice.
2. **Purpose limitation** — collect for specified, legitimate purposes only.
3. **Data minimisation** — collect only what is necessary.
4. **Accuracy** — keep data accurate and up to date.
5. **Storage limitation** — retain no longer than necessary.
6. **Integrity & confidentiality** — protect with appropriate security.
7. **Accountability** — demonstrate compliance.

## 5. Legal Basis / Consent

- A documented legal basis (consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interest) is required for each processing activity.
- Under PDPA, obtain and record consent (or rely on a valid exception); honour withdrawal of consent.

## 6. Data Subject Rights

Aspire honours applicable rights, including:
- Access and correction (PDPA)
- Access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, objection (GDPR)

Requests are logged and fulfilled within statutory timeframes ([30 days PDPA] / [1 month GDPR]).

## 7. Notice & Transparency

A clear privacy notice describes what data is collected, why, how it is used, who it is shared with, and how to exercise rights.

## 8. Cross-Border Transfers

- Personal data transferred outside the origin jurisdiction must have comparable protection (e.g. PDPA Transfer Limitation; GDPR adequacy / SCCs / BCRs).

## 9. Data Protection by Design & DPIAs

- Privacy is considered in the design of new products and processes.
- Data Protection Impact Assessments are conducted for high-risk processing.

## 10. Vendors & Processors

- Processors are bound by data processing agreements with security and confidentiality obligations (see Policy 07).

## 11. Breach Notification

- Personal data breaches are managed under the Incident Response Policy (Policy 08) and notified to regulators and affected individuals within statutory deadlines (e.g. PDPC notification, GDPR 72-hour rule).

## 12. Retention

Personal data is retained per a documented retention schedule and securely disposed of thereafter.

## 13. Roles

- **DPO** oversees privacy compliance and is the contact for regulators and data subjects.
- **All staff** handle personal data per this policy.

## 14. Enforcement & Review

Violations may result in disciplinary action and regulatory penalties. Reviewed at least annually.

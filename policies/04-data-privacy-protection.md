## 1. Purpose

To define how Aspire collects, uses, discloses, stores, transfers, and protects personal data in compliance with the Singapore Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) 2012, the EU GDPR where applicable, and the data-protection laws of each operating market. As a business-finance platform, Aspire processes significant volumes of personal and financial data about customers, their directors and beneficial owners, employees, and prospects; this policy governs that processing end-to-end.

## 2. Scope

All personal data processed by Aspire about customers, prospective customers, business representatives, employees, contractors, and other individuals, in any jurisdiction and in any form, whether Aspire acts as controller or processor.

## 5. Data Protection Principles

1. **Lawfulness, fairness, transparency** — process on a valid legal basis with clear notice.
2. **Purpose limitation** — collect for specified, legitimate purposes only.
3. **Data minimisation** — collect only what is necessary.
4. **Accuracy** — keep data accurate and current.
5. **Storage limitation** — retain no longer than necessary (Records Management the relevant policy).
6. **Integrity & confidentiality** — protect with appropriate security (Policies 01, 03, 11).
7. **Accountability** — demonstrate compliance through records and governance.

## 6. Lawful Basis & Consent (Procedure)

- A documented lawful basis (consent, contract, legal obligation, legitimate interests) is recorded in the RoPA for each processing activity.
- Under PDPA, obtain and record consent (or rely on a valid exception such as the legitimate interests or business improvement exceptions); honour withdrawal of consent promptly.
- Where AML/CFT or other legal obligations require processing, the legal-obligation basis applies and overrides consent withdrawal for that purpose.

## 7. Data Subject Rights (Procedure)

Aspire honours applicable rights:
- **PDPA:** access and correction.
- **GDPR:** access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection.

Requests are logged, identity-verified, and fulfilled within statutory timeframes (30 days PDPA / 1 month GDPR). The DPSR & DPIA Procedure (see Policy Index) details the workflow, exemptions (e.g. legal hold, AML retention), and escalation.

## 8. Notice & Transparency

A clear, layered privacy notice describes what data is collected, the purposes, legal bases, recipients, retention, cross-border transfers, and how to exercise rights and lodge complaints.

## 9. Cross-Border Transfers

Personal data transferred outside the origin jurisdiction must have comparable protection — PDPA Transfer Limitation Obligation; GDPR adequacy, SCCs, or BCRs — with transfer-impact assessments where required. Transfers are recorded in the RoPA.

## 10. Privacy by Design & DPIAs

- Privacy is embedded in the design of new products, features, and vendor engagements.
- A DPIA is conducted for high-risk processing (large-scale profiling, biometrics, new data sources) per the DPSR & DPIA Procedure (see Policy Index).

## 11. Vendors & Processors

Processors are bound by data processing agreements with security, confidentiality, sub-processor, and breach-notification obligations (Policy 07, Outsourcing Standard the relevant policy).

## 12. Breach Notification

Personal-data breaches are managed under the Incident Response Policy (Policy 08): assessed for risk of harm, and notified to the PDPC and affected individuals where required (PDPA notifiable-breach thresholds), and to the GDPR supervisory authority within 72 hours where applicable.

## 14. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

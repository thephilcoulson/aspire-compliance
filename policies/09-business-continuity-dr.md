## 1. Purpose

To ensure Aspire can continue or rapidly resume critical operations during and after a disruptive event, protecting customers, staff, and regulatory obligations.

## 2. Scope

All critical business functions, supporting systems, facilities, and key third parties.

## 3. Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

- Identify critical processes and their dependencies.
- Define for each critical service:
  - **RTO** (Recovery Time Objective) — maximum tolerable downtime.
  - **RPO** (Recovery Point Objective) — maximum tolerable data loss.
- Maintain a current BIA register reviewed at least annually.

## 4. Continuity Strategies

- Redundancy and resilience for critical infrastructure (multi-AZ/region as appropriate).
- Documented manual workarounds for key processes.
- Alternate work arrangements for staff (remote-capable).
- Continuity provisions for critical vendors (link to Policy 07).

## 5. Backup & Data Recovery

- Critical data backed up per defined schedule; backups encrypted and access-controlled.
- Backups stored in geographically separate locations.
- Restoration tested periodically to validate integrity and meet RPO.

## 6. Disaster Recovery (DR)

- DR plans and runbooks maintained for critical systems.
- Failover/recovery procedures documented and assigned to owners.

## 7. Crisis Management

- A crisis management team and escalation tree are defined.
- Communication plans cover staff, customers, regulators, and partners.

## 8. Testing & Exercises

- BCP/DR tested at least annually (tabletop and/or technical failover).
- Test results, gaps, and corrective actions documented and tracked.

## 9. Roles

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| BCP Coordinator | Maintains plans, schedules tests |
| Recovery Teams | Execute recovery procedures |
| Executive Sponsor | Activation decisions, external comms |

## 10. Enforcement & Review

Reviewed at least annually and after activation or significant change.

## 1. Purpose

To ensure all staff understand their compliance, financial-crime, security, privacy, and conduct obligations through role-appropriate training and ongoing awareness, building a strong compliance culture.

## 2. Scope

All employees, contractors, and (where relevant) directors and third-party personnel.

## 5. Training Curriculum

All staff complete induction training on onboarding and **annual** refreshers covering: the Code of Conduct (Policy 16), information security and acceptable use (Policies 01, 05), data privacy (Policy 04), and AML/CFT awareness. Role-based training applies to higher-risk roles — e.g. detailed AML/CFT and sanctions for financial-crime, onboarding, and operations staff (Policy 12); secure development for engineers (Policy 31); privacy for data-handling teams.

## 6. Cadence & Triggers

Training is delivered on onboarding (within 30 days), annually thereafter, and on material regulatory or policy change. New high-risk role assignments trigger role-based training before the role commences.

## 7. Awareness Activities

Ongoing awareness includes security/phishing campaigns, **quarterly phishing simulations**, intranet updates, and targeted communications on emerging threats and typologies.

## 8. Completion Tracking & Attestation

Completion and policy attestations are tracked centrally; targets are enforced (e.g. ≥ 98–99% completion). Non-completion is escalated to managers and may affect access or be treated as a conduct matter.

## 9. Effectiveness

Training effectiveness is assessed (e.g. assessment scores, phishing-failure trends) and used to improve content; results are reported to management and feed the compliance program (Policy 00).

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

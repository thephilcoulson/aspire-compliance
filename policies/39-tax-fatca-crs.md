## 1. Purpose

To ensure Aspire meets its tax-transparency obligations, including customer tax-residency identification, due diligence, and reporting under the Common Reporting Standard (CRS) and the US Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA), and broader tax-compliance obligations.

## 2. Scope

All reportable financial accounts and customers in scope of CRS/FATCA, and Aspire's own tax-compliance obligations, across applicable markets.

## 5. Customer Tax Due Diligence

At onboarding, Aspire collects valid self-certifications of tax residency and TINs, and reviews for indicia of reportable status. Documentation is validated for reasonableness against other KYC data (Policy 13); changes in circumstances trigger re-certification.

## 6. Identifying Reportable Accounts

Accounts are classified per FATCA/CRS rules to identify reportable persons/entities (including controlling persons of passive entities). The reportable population is maintained and reconciled annually.

## 7. Reporting Procedure

1. Compile the reportable population for the reporting period.
2. Validate data completeness (TINs, balances, payments).
3. Prepare returns in the required format.
4. File with the relevant authority (e.g. via IRAS for Singapore) by the statutory deadline.
5. Retain evidence of filing (Policy 26).

## 8. Withholding (FATCA)

Where FATCA withholding obligations apply (e.g. on recalcitrant/non-participating cases), they are assessed and applied per the rules, with documentation retained.

## 9. Governance & Records

Tax-compliance obligations are tracked in the Regulatory Obligations Register (Policy 17); records are retained per local tax law (commonly 5–7 years; confirmed per jurisdiction) (Policy 26).

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

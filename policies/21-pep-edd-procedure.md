## 1. Purpose

To define how Aspire identifies, risk-assesses, approves, and monitors Politically Exposed Persons and other higher-risk customers requiring Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD), in support of the AML/CFT (12) and KYC/KYB (13) policies.

## 2. Scope

All customers, beneficial owners, and connected parties who are PEPs or otherwise higher-risk, across all products and markets.

## 5. PEP Identification

PEP status is determined at onboarding and ongoing via screening against PEP lists, customer declarations, and adverse-media checks. The definition covers foreign, domestic, and international-organisation PEPs and their relatives and close associates (RCAs). PEP status is risk-based (not automatic decline) but always triggers EDD and senior approval.

## 6. EDD Triggers

EDD applies to: PEPs/RCAs; high-risk jurisdictions; complex/opaque ownership; cash-intensive or high-risk industries; unusual transaction profiles; adverse media; and any customer rated High. The trigger and rationale are documented.

## 7. EDD Measures (Procedure)

1. Establish source of funds and source of wealth with corroborating evidence.
2. Obtain additional identity/ownership information.
3. Conduct enhanced adverse-media and litigation checks.
4. Document the business rationale for the relationship.
5. Obtain senior-management approval before onboarding/continuation.
6. Apply enhanced ongoing monitoring and more frequent reviews.

## 8. Senior Management Approval

No PEP relationship is established or continued without documented senior-management approval. Approval records the rationale, the EDD findings, and the assigned review frequency.

## 9. Enhanced Ongoing Monitoring

Higher-risk/PEP relationships are subject to closer transaction monitoring (Policy the relevant policy) and more frequent periodic reviews (e.g. at least annually), with re-approval where risk materially changes.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

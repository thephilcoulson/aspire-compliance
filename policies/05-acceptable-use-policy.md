## 1. Purpose

To define acceptable use of Aspire's information systems, devices, networks, applications, and data, and to protect against misuse that could harm the Company, its customers, its partners, or its staff. Given Aspire's handling of customer funds and sensitive financial data, disciplined use of Company assets is a core control.

## 2. Scope

All users of Aspire systems and assets — employees, contractors, interns, and authorised third parties — and all devices (Company-owned and approved BYOD) used to access Company resources.

## 5. General Conduct

- Use Company assets for legitimate business purposes; limited reasonable personal use is permitted where it does not interfere with duties, security, or capacity.
- Do not use systems for illegal, harassing, discriminatory, defamatory, or fraudulent activity.

## 6. Account & Credential Use

- Keep credentials confidential; never share passwords or MFA tokens.
- Do not attempt to access data or systems beyond your authorisation.
- Lock screens when unattended; comply with clear-desk / clear-screen practice.

## 7. Acceptable Device Use

- Company and approved BYOD devices accessing Company data must meet security baselines (full-disk encryption, screen lock, current OS/patches, endpoint protection, MDM enrolment) per the Mobile, Remote Working & BYOD policy (see Policy Index).
- Do not disable security controls (anti-malware, MDM, firewalls, DLP).

## 8. Data Handling

- Handle data per the Data Classification Policy (03).
- Do not store Restricted/Confidential data on unauthorised cloud services or personal devices.
- Do not transmit Restricted/Confidential data over unapproved channels.

## 9. Email, Messaging & Internet

- Be vigilant against phishing, business-email-compromise, and social engineering; report suspicious messages via reporting channel/button.
- Do not auto-forward Company email to personal accounts.

## 10. Software & Resources

- Install only approved, licensed software; no unauthorised or pirated software.
- Do not introduce malware or unauthorised code, or circumvent licensing.

## 11. AI & Third-Party Tools

- Do not input Restricted or Confidential data into unapproved external AI/SaaS tools.
- Use only sanctioned tools for processing Company or customer data, consistent with the AI Governance & Responsible Use policy (see Policy Index).

## 12. Monitoring

Aspire may monitor use of its systems for security, capacity, and compliance purposes, subject to applicable law and employee privacy notices. Monitoring is proportionate and documented.

## 13. Prohibited Activities

Unauthorised access, data exfiltration, crypto-mining, hosting unauthorised services, bypassing security controls, disabling logging, or using Company assets to attack third parties are prohibited.

## 15. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

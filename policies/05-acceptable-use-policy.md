## 1. Purpose

To define acceptable use of Aspire's information systems, devices, networks, and data, and protect against misuse that could harm the Company, its customers, or staff.

## 2. Scope

All users of Aspire systems and assets, including employees, contractors, and third parties, and all devices used to access Company resources.

## 3. General Conduct

- Use Company assets for legitimate business purposes; limited reasonable personal use is permitted where it does not interfere with duties or security.
- Do not use systems for illegal, harassing, discriminatory, or fraudulent activity.

## 4. Account & Credential Use

- Keep credentials confidential; never share passwords or MFA tokens.
- Do not attempt to access data or systems beyond your authorisation.
- Lock screens when unattended; comply with clear-desk/clear-screen practices.

## 5. Acceptable Device Use

- Company and BYOD devices accessing Company data must meet security baselines (encryption, screen lock, up-to-date OS, endpoint protection).
- Do not disable security controls (antivirus, MDM, firewalls).

## 6. Data Handling

- Handle data per the Data Classification Policy (Policy 03).
- Do not store Restricted/Confidential data on unauthorised cloud services or personal devices.

## 7. Email, Messaging & Internet

- Be vigilant against phishing and social engineering; report suspicious messages.
- Do not transmit Restricted/Confidential data over unapproved channels.

## 8. Software & Resources

- Install only approved/licensed software.
- Do not introduce malware or unauthorised code; do not circumvent licensing.

## 9. AI & Third-Party Tools

- Do not input Restricted or Confidential data into unapproved external AI/SaaS tools.
- Use only sanctioned tools for processing Company or customer data.

## 10. Monitoring

Aspire may monitor use of its systems for security and compliance, subject to applicable law and privacy notices.

## 11. Prohibited Activities

- Unauthorised access, data exfiltration, crypto-mining, hosting unauthorised services, or bypassing security controls.

## 12. Enforcement & Review

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to termination and legal action. Reviewed at least annually.

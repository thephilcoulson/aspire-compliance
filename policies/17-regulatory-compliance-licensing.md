## 1. Purpose

To ensure Aspire obtains, maintains, and complies with all licences, registrations, and authorisations required to operate its business-finance products across each jurisdiction, and that regulatory obligations and changes are tracked and discharged. As a multi-market fintech, Aspire's licence to operate depends on disciplined regulatory management.

## 2. Scope

All entities, products, channels, and markets, and all activities that are licensable or notifiable (payments, e-money, lending, money-changing/remittance, card issuing/acquiring).

## 5. Licensing Inventory & Permitted Activities

Maintain a live inventory of every licence/registration held, the authorised activities, conditions, key-person requirements, capital/financial requirements, and renewal/reporting deadlines. Each product is mapped to the licence(s) that permit it; activities outside permitted scope are prohibited until authorisation is obtained.

## 6. Licence Application & Variation Procedure

1. Identify the licensable activity and target jurisdiction.
2. Conduct a regulatory gap assessment.
3. Prepare the application (business plan, governance, AML/CFT framework, fit-&-proper documentation).
4. Submit and manage regulator queries.
5. Implement licence conditions before go-live.
6. Record the authorisation and its conditions in the inventory.

## 7. Ongoing Obligations & Notifications

Track and meet ongoing obligations: periodic regulatory returns, capital/financial-resource requirements, change-in-control and key-appointment notifications, outsourcing notifications, and incident reporting. A regulatory calendar drives reminders and ownership for each deadline.

## 8. Horizon Scanning & Regulatory Change

Compliance and Legal monitor regulatory developments (MAS, FATF, and local regulators), assess impact, and assign implementation actions with owners and due dates, recorded in the Regulatory Obligations Register.

## 9. Regulatory Engagement

All regulator interactions are coordinated through Compliance/Legal, logged, and conducted with transparency and timeliness. Material communications and commitments are recorded and tracked to closure.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

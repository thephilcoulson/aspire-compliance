## 1. Purpose

To ensure procurement is conducted ethically, competitively, and with appropriate risk controls, and to set the standards Aspire expects of its suppliers (ethics, anti-bribery, security, data protection, labour, and sustainability), complementing the Vendor Risk Policy (07).

## 2. Scope

All procurement activity and all suppliers/vendors engaged by Aspire.

## 5. Procurement Principles

Procurement is conducted with integrity, fairness, and value for money. Material spend uses competitive sourcing with documented selection criteria. Procurement decisions are free from conflicts of interest (Policy 23) and bribery (Policy 24); related-party situations require independent approval.

## 6. Supplier Code of Conduct

Suppliers are expected to: act ethically and comply with law; prohibit bribery/corruption; protect Aspire and customer data and meet security requirements (Policies 01, 04, 07); respect labour rights and prohibit modern slavery/child labour; meet health-and-safety standards; and operate responsibly on environmental/sustainability matters. The code is incorporated by reference into supplier contracts.

## 7. Supplier Onboarding & Risk

Before engagement, suppliers are risk-assessed (security, data, financial-crime, ABC, financial stability) per the Vendor & Third-Party Risk Policy (07); higher-risk suppliers undergo enhanced due diligence and contractual controls (audit rights, DPAs, ABC clauses).

## 8. Contracting Controls

Contracts include the supplier code, security and data-protection requirements, ABC representations, audit/termination rights, and exit provisions. Maker-checker and appropriate authority approve contract execution.

## 9. Monitoring & Breach

Supplier compliance is monitored over the lifecycle (Policy 07); material breaches of the code trigger remediation or termination.

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

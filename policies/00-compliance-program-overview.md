## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the overarching compliance and governance framework for Aspire ("the Company"). It defines how the Company identifies applicable obligations, assigns accountability, and maintains an effective, auditable control environment across all jurisdictions in which it operates.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors, directors, officers, and third parties acting on behalf of Aspire, across all entities, products, and markets.

## 3. Regulatory & Standards Landscape

Aspire operates as a regulated financial services provider. Applicable obligations include, but are not limited to:

- **Singapore:** Payment Services Act (PSA) 2019; MAS Notices PSN01 (AML/CFT) and PSN02; PDPA 2012.
- **AML/CFT:** FATF 40 Recommendations; local AML/CFT acts in each operating market.
- **Information Security:** ISO/IEC 27001:2022; SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria.
- **Payments:** PCI-DSS v4.0 where cardholder data is processed, stored, or transmitted.
- **Privacy:** Singapore PDPA; EU GDPR where EU personal data is processed.

> Maintain a live **Regulatory Obligations Register** mapping each obligation to a responsible owner and the controls that satisfy it.

## 4. Governance Structure

| Body / Role | Responsibility |
|-------------|----------------|
| Board / Audit & Risk Committee | Ultimate accountability for compliance; approves risk appetite. |
| Chief Compliance Officer (CCO) | Owns the compliance program; reports to the Board. |
| Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO) | Owns AML/CFT program; files suspicious transaction reports. |
| Data Protection Officer (DPO) | Owns privacy compliance (PDPA/GDPR). |
| Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) | Owns the ISMS and security controls. |
| Department Heads | Implement controls within their functions. |
| All Staff | Comply with policies; report concerns. |

## 5. Three Lines of Defence

1. **First line** — business units own and manage their risks and controls.
2. **Second line** — Compliance and Risk functions set policy, monitor, and challenge.
3. **Third line** — Internal Audit provides independent assurance to the Board.

## 6. Policy Management

- All policies are version-controlled, reviewed at least annually, and formally approved.
- Material changes require re-approval by the accountable owner and, where relevant, the Board.
- Policies are accessible to all staff and acknowledged on hire and after material updates.

## 7. Training & Awareness

- Mandatory compliance training on onboarding and at least annually thereafter.
- Role-specific training (AML, security, privacy) for relevant functions.
- Completion tracked and reported to the CCO.

## 8. Monitoring, Testing & Reporting

- Compliance maintains a monitoring plan covering all key controls.
- Findings are logged, risk-rated, remediated, and tracked to closure.
- Periodic compliance reports are delivered to the Board/Audit & Risk Committee.

## 9. Breaches & Enforcement

- Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action up to termination, and regulatory or legal consequences.
- Suspected breaches must be reported via the channels in the Code of Conduct (Policy 16).

## 10. Whistleblowing

A confidential, non-retaliatory channel is maintained for reporting suspected misconduct. See the Code of Conduct (Policy 16).

## 11. Review

This policy is reviewed at least annually and upon material regulatory or business change.

## 1. Purpose

This document establishes the overarching compliance and governance framework for Aspire ("the Company"), a Southeast Asian business-finance platform providing business accounts, payments, corporate cards, expense management, and credit products. It defines how the Company identifies applicable legal and regulatory obligations, assigns clear accountability, operates an effective and auditable control environment, and continuously improves that environment across every jurisdiction in which it operates.

The framework exists to:

- Provide a single, board-endorsed reference for how compliance is governed at Aspire.
- Ensure obligations under financial-services, AML/CFT, data-protection, and information-security regimes are systematically tracked and discharged.
- Establish the relationship between this overview and the detailed downstream policies (Policies 01–the relevant policy).
- Demonstrate to regulators, partners (banks, card schemes, payment networks), auditors, and customers that Aspire operates a credible, risk-based compliance program.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, secondees, interns, directors, officers, and third parties acting on behalf of Aspire, across all legal entities, products, channels, and operating markets (Singapore as the primary regulated hub and all current and future expansion markets in Southeast Asia and beyond).

It governs the full compliance lifecycle: obligation identification, policy authorship and approval, control implementation, monitoring and testing, issue management, regulatory engagement, and reporting. Where a downstream policy provides more specific requirements, that policy prevails on the specific matter, but this overview governs the structure within which all such policies operate.

## 4. Regulatory & Standards Landscape

Aspire operates as a regulated financial-services provider and an information-handling business. Applicable obligations include, but are not limited to:

- **Singapore:** Payment Services Act (PSA) 2019; MAS Notice PSN01 (Prevention of Money Laundering and Countering the Financing of Terrorism) and PSN02; MAS Technology Risk Management (TRM) Guidelines; MAS Guidelines on Outsourcing; MAS Notice on Cyber Hygiene; Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) 2012.
- **AML/CFT:** FATF 40 Recommendations; the AML/CFT statutes of each operating market; UN, OFAC, EU, and MAS sanctions regimes.
- **Information Security:** ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (ISMS); SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security, Availability, Confidentiality, Processing Integrity, Privacy).
- **Payments:** PCI-DSS v4.0 wherever cardholder data is stored, processed, or transmitted; applicable card-scheme operating rules.
- **Privacy:** Singapore PDPA; the data-protection laws of each operating market; EU GDPR where EU personal data is processed.
- **Tax transparency:** FATCA and CRS where applicable.

> Aspire shall maintain a live **Regulatory Obligations Register** mapping each obligation to a responsible owner, the controls that satisfy it, the evidence demonstrating compliance, and the review date. The register is reviewed at least quarterly and upon any material regulatory change (horizon scanning).

## 6. Three Lines of Defence

1. **First line — business and operations:** Own and manage the risks in their day-to-day activities; design and run controls; remediate issues.
2. **Second line — Compliance and Risk:** Set policy and standards, provide expertise, monitor and challenge the first line, and aggregate reporting.
3. **Third line — Internal Audit:** Provides independent, objective assurance to the Board over the design and operating effectiveness of governance, risk management, and control.

External assurance (external auditors, ISO/SOC 2 assessors, QSAs, and the regulator) sits outside but complements the three lines.

## 7. Policy Management Lifecycle

- **Authorship:** Each policy has a single accountable owner identified in its Document Control table.
- **Approval:** Policies are formally approved by the owner and, where material, the Board/Audit & Risk Committee before becoming operational.
- **Versioning:** All policies are version-controlled in this repository; the git history serves as the change log; material changes are approved via pull request.
- **Review:** Every policy is reviewed at least annually and upon material regulatory, business, or threat-landscape change.
- **Accessibility & acknowledgement:** Policies are accessible to all staff and acknowledged on hire and after material updates.
- **Hierarchy:** Policy (board-level intent) → Standard (mandatory technical/operational requirements) → Procedure (step-by-step how-to) → Guideline (recommended practice).

## 8. Compliance Operating Procedures

1. **Horizon scanning:** Compliance and Legal monitor regulatory developments across all markets and log changes in the Regulatory Obligations Register.
2. **Impact assessment:** Each change is assessed for impact on products, processes, and controls; actions are assigned with owners and due dates.
3. **Control mapping:** Each obligation is mapped to one or more controls and to the evidence that demonstrates the control operates.
4. **Monitoring & testing:** Compliance executes a risk-based annual Compliance Monitoring Plan, testing the design and operation of key controls.
5. **Issue & action management:** Findings are logged, risk-rated, assigned, and tracked to closure in the GRC tool.
6. **Reporting:** A periodic compliance report (at least quarterly) is delivered to the Board/Audit & Risk Committee, covering obligations, issues, KPIs, incidents, and regulatory engagement.
7. **Regulatory engagement:** All regulator interactions are coordinated through Compliance/Legal and documented.

## 10. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

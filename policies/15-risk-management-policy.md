## 1. Purpose

To establish a consistent framework for identifying, assessing, treating, and monitoring risks across Aspire — including operational, security, compliance, financial, and strategic risks.

## 2. Scope

All risks affecting Aspire's objectives, assets, customers, and regulatory standing.

## 3. Risk Governance

- The **Board / Audit & Risk Committee** sets risk appetite and oversees the framework.
- **Risk/Compliance functions** maintain the framework and risk register.
- **Risk owners** manage risks within their domain.

## 4. Risk Appetite

- The Board defines risk appetite and tolerance levels per risk category. Risks exceeding tolerance require treatment or explicit, documented acceptance.

## 5. Risk Management Process

1. **Identify** — capture risks from assessments, incidents, audits, and operations.
2. **Assess** — rate likelihood × impact to derive an inherent risk score.
3. **Treat** — choose mitigate / transfer / avoid / accept.
4. **Control** — implement controls; record residual risk.
5. **Monitor & Review** — track risks and control effectiveness over time.

## 6. Risk Assessment Scale (example)

| Likelihood \ Impact | Low | Medium | High |
|---------------------|-----|--------|------|
| Likely | Medium | High | Critical |
| Possible | Low | Medium | High |
| Unlikely | Low | Low | Medium |

## 7. Information Security Risk

- Security risk assessments feed the ISMS (Policy 01) and the Statement of Applicability for ISO 27001 control selection.

## 8. Risk Register

- A central risk register records each risk: description, owner, inherent/residual rating, treatment, controls, and review date.
- Reviewed at least quarterly by the risk function and reported to the Board.

## 9. Risk Acceptance

- Acceptance of risks above tolerance requires documented sign-off by an appropriate authority, with periodic re-review.

## 10. Enforcement & Review

Reviewed at least annually and upon material change to the business or threat landscape.

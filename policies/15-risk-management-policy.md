## 1. Purpose

To establish a consistent, enterprise-wide framework for identifying, assessing, treating, monitoring, and reporting risks across Aspire — including operational, security, compliance, financial-crime, technology, conduct, financial, and strategic risks — aligned to the Board's risk appetite.

## 2. Scope

All risks affecting Aspire's objectives, assets, customers, funds, and regulatory standing, across all entities and markets.

## 5. Risk Appetite

The Board defines risk appetite and tolerance per risk category (e.g. zero appetite for regulatory non-compliance and financial-crime facilitation; defined tolerances for operational and technology risk). Risks exceeding tolerance require treatment or explicit, documented acceptance.

## 6. Risk Management Process (Procedure)

1. **Identify** — capture risks from assessments, RCSAs, incidents, audits, KRIs, and operations.
2. **Assess** — rate likelihood × impact to derive an inherent score.
3. **Treat** — choose mitigate / transfer / avoid / accept.
4. **Control** — implement controls; record residual risk.
5. **Monitor & Review** — track risks, controls, and KRIs over time.
6. **Report** — escalate and report to the Board/ARC.

## 8. Information Security & Other Risk Domains

## 9. Risk Register

A central risk register records each risk: description, owner, category, inherent/residual rating, treatment, controls, KRIs, and review date. It is reviewed at least quarterly by the risk function and reported to the Board.

## 10. Risk Acceptance

Acceptance of risks above tolerance requires documented sign-off by an appropriate authority, with a defined expiry and periodic re-review (aligns with the Exceptions process below).

## 12. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the GRC / exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.

## 1. Purpose

To establish Aspire's Information Security Management System (ISMS) and define the principles, responsibilities, and controls that protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA) of information assets.

## 2. Scope

All information assets owned or processed by Aspire, including customer data, financial data, source code, infrastructure, and personnel — regardless of location or media.

## 3. Information Security Objectives

- Protect customer and corporate data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction.
- Ensure regulatory and contractual security obligations are met.
- Maintain availability of critical services in line with business needs.
- Continuously improve the ISMS based on risk and incident learnings.

## 4. ISMS Governance

- The CISO owns the ISMS and reports security posture to executive leadership.
- An Information Security Committee meets at least quarterly to review risks, incidents, and metrics.
- Security risk is managed under the Risk Management Policy (Policy 15).

## 5. Core Control Domains (ISO 27001 Annex A aligned)

| Domain | Summary |
|--------|---------|
| Organisational controls | Policies, roles, threat intel, supplier security, continuity. |
| People controls | Screening, training, disciplinary process, NDAs. |
| Physical controls | Secure areas, equipment protection, clear desk/screen. |
| Technological controls | Access control, cryptography, logging, malware defence, secure development, network security. |

## 6. Key Requirements

- **Risk-based:** Controls are selected via a documented risk assessment and Statement of Applicability.
- **Least privilege:** Access is granted on need-to-know and least-privilege basis (see Policy 02).
- **Encryption:** Data is encrypted in transit and at rest (see Policy 11).
- **Logging & monitoring:** Security-relevant events are logged, protected, and monitored.
- **Secure SDLC:** Security is embedded across the software development lifecycle (see Policy 10).
- **Vulnerability management:** Systems are patched and scanned on a defined cadence; critical vulnerabilities remediated within defined SLAs.
- **Asset management:** All assets are inventoried with an assigned owner.

## 7. Acceptable Use

All users must comply with the Acceptable Use Policy (Policy 05).

## 8. Incident Management

Security incidents are handled under the Incident Response Policy (Policy 08).

## 9. Compliance & Audit

- The ISMS is subject to internal and external audit.
- Non-conformities are tracked to closure with corrective actions.

## 10. Continuous Improvement

The ISMS follows a Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle. Management reviews are conducted at least annually.

## 11. Enforcement

Violations may result in disciplinary action and, where applicable, legal or regulatory consequences.

## 12. Review

Reviewed at least annually and after any significant security incident or material change.

## 1. Purpose

To define the compliance controls across the customer lifecycle — from onboarding through ongoing servicing to offboarding/closure — ensuring KYC/KYB, screening, risk rating, disclosures, and exit are performed consistently and lawfully.

## 2. Scope

## 5. Onboarding Controls

At onboarding, Aspire performs KYC/KYB and beneficial-ownership verification (Policy 13), sanctions/PEP/adverse-media screening (Policies 18, 21), customer risk rating, and required disclosures/consents (Policy 04). Accounts are not activated until verification and screening clear; higher-risk cases require EDD and senior approval (Policy 21).

## 6. Customer Risk Rating

Each customer is risk-rated (Low/Medium/High) using customer, product, channel, and geographic factors. The rating drives diligence depth and review frequency.

## 7. Ongoing Reviews

Periodic KYC refresh occurs by risk tier — **High: annually; Medium: every 2 years; Low: every 3 years** — and on trigger events (ownership change, adverse media, unusual activity, sanctions developments). Reviews update verification, screening, and risk rating.

## 8. Servicing & Conduct

Throughout servicing, communications are clear and fair (Policy 22); changes (mandates, beneficial ownership, products) are controlled and re-screened where relevant.

## 9. Offboarding & De-risking

Relationships are closed for legitimate reasons (customer request, unacceptable risk, failure to complete CDD, sanctions). Exit follows a controlled process with notice where appropriate, retention of records (Policy 26), and STR consideration where suspicion exists (no tipping-off).

## 11. Exceptions & Waivers

Any deviation from this policy requires a formal, time-bound exception.

1. **Request:** The requester logs an exception in the the GRC exception register describing the control deviated from, the business justification, the compensating controls, and the requested expiry date.
2. **Risk assessment:** The relevant control owner and the Risk function assess the residual risk of the deviation.
3. **Approval authority:** Exceptions are approved by the Policy Owner for low/medium residual risk and by the the CISO/CCO and, where material, the Audit & Risk Committee for high residual risk.
4. **Time limits:** Exceptions are granted for a maximum of 90 days and must be re-reviewed before renewal. No exception is open-ended.
5. **Tracking:** All approved exceptions are recorded in the exception register with an owner, expiry, and remediation plan, and are reported in periodic compliance reporting.
6. **Revocation:** Exceptions may be revoked at any time if the residual risk changes or the compensating controls fail.
